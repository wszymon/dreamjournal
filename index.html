Have you heard stories of people finding great ideas in dreams, altering history? It's not just luck. Discoveries in dreams can change your life â€” if you can recall them.

Jotting down dreams in a notebook takes time we often lack upon waking. With our clear table, you can note dreams quickly and add details later. Don't let ideas fade. With our Dream Journal, turn dreams into inspiration, tapping into endless creativity.

How to fill the table?

To recall dreams, jot down a few keywords from key categories immediately upon waking.

See a sample page from our dream journal below.

  • 1. Location - specify the location where the dream takes place.
  • 2. Date - provide the date when you woke up after the dream.
  • 3. People - list the characters that appeared in your dream.
  • 4. Weather - what weather conditions or atmospheric phenomena accompany the dream events.
  • 5. Time of Day - indicate when the dream events occur.
  • 6. Awareness - determine the level of awareness you had in the dream.
  • 7. Fear - Was your dream scary?
  • 8. Keywords - add words that come to mind and may be helpful in describing the dream.
  • 9. Description - this area is open for you.
